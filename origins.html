The first swords appeared around 2000 BC during the Bronze age. Working with metals got way easier and longer blades were possible to make, turning daggers into the sword. This happened all over the world during the bronze age. This first sword was mainly a stabbing weapon with a small leaf shaped hilt.

Around the 13th century BC, the iron sword was created. It wasn't as good as the bronze sword, but it could be made in mass quantities. This meant that armies could be supplied with swords.

Around the 11th to 13th century AD, the cross guard was added to protect the holder's hands. The swords of this time were both single edged and double edged and were mainly used as cutting weapons.

Because armor was quickly getting better, swords needed to get better too. Steel swords were made as well as swords that could cut and thrust; seeking out weaknesses in armor.
